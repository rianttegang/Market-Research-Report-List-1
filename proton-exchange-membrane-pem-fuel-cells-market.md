<p><p>The Proton Exchange Membrane (PEM) Fuel Cells market encompasses three main hydrogen storage types: compressed gaseous hydrogen, cryogenic liquid hydrogen, and hydrides. Compressed gaseous hydrogen stores hydrogen at high pressure, facilitating quick refueling and portability. Cryogenic liquid hydrogen involves cooling hydrogen to extremely low temperatures for efficient storage and transport. Hydrides, on the other hand, utilize metal alloys to absorb and release hydrogen, providing a safer, more compact storage solution. Each type caters to varying application needs and infrastructure capabilities.</p></p>
