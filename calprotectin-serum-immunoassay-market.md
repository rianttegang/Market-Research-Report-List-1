<p><p>The Calprotectin serum immunoassay market includes several assay types, each employing different methodologies. ELISA (Enzyme Linked Immunosorbent Assay) uses colorimetric detection to quantify calprotectin levels, offering high specificity and sensitivity. Enzyme Fluoroimmunoassay leverages fluorescent labels to enhance detection capabilities and provide rapid results. Quantitative Immune-chromatography uses a simple, rapid testing format that allows for point-of-care testing, facilitating immediate clinical decision-making. Each method serves different clinical needs, emphasizing accuracy and efficiency in monitoring inflammatory conditions.</p></p>
