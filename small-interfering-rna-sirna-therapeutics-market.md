<p><p>Small Interfering RNA (siRNA) therapeutics are approaches for gene silencing that leverage different delivery systems. Liposome-based systemic therapy utilizes lipid vesicles to encapsulate siRNA, promoting cellular uptake and reducing degradation. Nanoparticle-based systemic therapy employs nanoscale carriers, enhancing stability and targeted delivery to specific tissues or cells. Other market types may include viral vectors or polymer-based systems, each offering unique advantages in terms of stability, targeting efficiency, and payload capacity for effective gene regulation and therapeutic applications.</p></p>
