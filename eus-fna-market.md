<p><ul><li>Core Puncture</li><li>Edge Puncture</li></ul></p>
<p><p>EUS-FNA (Endoscopic Ultrasound-Guided Fine Needle Aspiration) involves two main market types: core puncture and edge puncture. Core puncture utilizes larger needles to obtain tissue samples, providing more substantial and diagnostic-quality specimens, which is crucial for accurate evaluations of lesions. In contrast, edge puncture employs thinner needles, focusing on fluid aspiration and smaller tissue samples. While core puncture is favored for its diagnostic yield, edge puncture is often preferred for less invasive procedures, catering to different clinical needs and preferences.</p></p>
