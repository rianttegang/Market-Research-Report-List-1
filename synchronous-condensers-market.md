<p><p>Synchronous condensers are devices used to enhance power system stability and improve voltage regulation by supplying reactive power. The market is categorized into hydrogen, air, and water synchronous condensers. Hydrogen synchronous condensers utilize hydrogen for cooling and offer high efficiency, while air synchronous condensers use ambient air, making them simpler but potentially less efficient. Water synchronous condensers leverage water for cooling, providing effective temperature control but requiring more infrastructure. Each type serves distinct operational needs and environmental conditions within power systems.</p></p>
