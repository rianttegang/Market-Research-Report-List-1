<p><p>The microtube centrifuge market is categorized into two main types: refrigerated and non-refrigerated. Refrigerated microtube centrifuges maintain low temperatures during centrifugation, which is essential for temperature-sensitive samples, helping to preserve biological activity and integrity. In contrast, non-refrigerated microtube centrifuges operate at ambient temperatures and are typically used for routine applications where temperature control is not critical. Both types cater to different laboratory needs, influencing their adoption across research, clinical, and industrial sectors.</p></p>
